Digital Transformation

What is Digital Transformation?

Organizational investment in technology requires strategic adoption of the right tool, applied to the right use case, and implemented with flexibility in order to rapidly adopt newer capabilities that offer improved work experiences and efficiencies.

Today, users are riding a continuous technology wave driven by a desire to improve user experiences and expect systems that bear more functionality and intelligence as more operations move to the cloud. The antiquated models based on rigid processes supported by legacy IT environments now limit the ability for an enterprise to respond in todayâ€™s rapidly changing environment.

Digital Transformation starts by understanding the implications of social, mobile, analytic, cloud technologies and user experience (SMAC-X) and exploring the requirements of the enterprise to get more work done and at lower costs than traditional operating models. Agencies are using digital advances offered by advanced web applications, cloud-based analytics, mobile solutions, and smart devices to gain operational efficiencies.

With the advent of these digital capabilities and the continual evolution of newer digital ecosystems, digital transformation seeks to eliminate stove-piped capabilities and simplify the volume, redundancy, and complexity of distributed architectures that are closed and proprietary through modernization of the IT portfolio with technologies that increase capabilities through open architectures that promote interoperability, open source components that maximize reusability, open standards that foster cohesive development, open communities that fosters sharing and innovation, and open data that make information available to users.

Social

The collective of online communications channels dedicated to communitybased input, interaction and collaboration

Mobile

Store, access, create, modify, organize, or otherwise manipulate data without being tethered to any particular spot

Analytics

Driving insights through the systematic, computational analysis of data sets or statistics

Cloud

A network of remote servers hosted on the Internet, rather than a local server or personal computer, to store, manage, and process data

Experience

The overall experience of a person using a product, such as a website or computer application, especially in terms of how easy or pleasing it is to operate

Digital transformation is also a profound and continuous evolution of business activities, processes, and competencies that enhance an organizationâ€™s ability to adapt to change. It is a journey that uses disruptive digital technologies as enablers to innovation with faster impact and with flexibility to quickly change when better, more efficient capabilities become available. As digital capabilities are introduced, business activities shift to become fast, virtual, and flexible. Interactions, tasks, and work processes are undergoing significant transformations as new technology allows information to be created, exchanged, secured, and processed in very different ways.

On-demand engagement, heightened user experience, remote data access, and cloudbased analytics are the new capability standards which organizations must begin to transform their workforce and operations into to become effective digital workplaces.

To accommodate these changes, digital transformation also demands innovative organizational strategies, including how to innovate and differentiate from other organizations. These strategies should include organizational change to support new business objectives and the integration of digital experts as key enablers to develop purpose built solutions, monitor the new digital trends, and present fresh innovations for adoption.
